ENABLE -- iGEM Fudan

Introduction

In the year of 2018, I participated in the iGEM compitition (International Genetically Engineered Machine) on behalf of Fudan University. Working as a team member, my major job was to present a full set of brand identity design, which includes the LOGO, website, and team uniform, etc. We earned silver medal in the final evaluation.

Before it comes to the graphic design itself, I would like to firstly introduce our project in a scientific way. We all know that, to survive, cells must identify and integrate various signals from the environment to decide actions like tissue formation or cell-mediated immunity. Therefore, in the field of synthetic biology, it would be especially impactful if cells could be programmed to recognize external environmental signals, generate contact-dependent signaling, and perform reliable logical computations. Our project ENABLE (ENgineered, Across membrane, Binary Logic in Eukaryotes) offers the first toolbox for mammalian cells to generate contact-dependent signaling from the external environment and Boolean output decisions. We believe this is a foundational advance for synthetic biology, and enables a wide range of opportunities.

In the whole project, there are two things that I am proud of most. They are LOGO design and Bio-Art display.


LOGO design

The logo aims to reflect the theme of our project that is transmembrane binary logic. I used three polygons to represent three cells, with two on the left providing signals to the hero cell on the right–who integrates the information and make the final output decision. The signals are indicated using arrows. The three colors used throughout our whole project. They are bright and attactive but also balanced ,symbolizing that synthetic biology not only relies on biological experiments, but also requires contributions of various other fields such as computer science, mathematics, sociology, etc.



Bio-Art display

Bio-Art display is one of our way to carry out the public engagement, which combines synthetic biology and art to let people appreciate synthetic biology and understand more about our projects.

It is always hard for our team to explain what exactly “binary logic” is and how it can be built in cells. So in the display, I decided to provide audience the chance to physically analyze and construct a sample logic gate using constructible electrical wire circuits, electrical boards and lightbulbs! Besides of that part, I also designed the “interactive lab”, which allowed people to perform some basic scientific experiments using the laboratory equipments that were sterilized beforehands.

To help the audience navigate the display, there were a lot of humanized details in the exhibition. For example, we designed small note papers, on which the audience could write down their thoughts at any time throughout the display. In each exhibition area, our team members were always present to help explain the project and also communicate with the audience. I also carefully designed the guidebook, which provides a brief explanation of the contents of the exhibition.
