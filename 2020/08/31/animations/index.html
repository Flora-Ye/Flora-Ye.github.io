Vesicle Fusion

Three Modes of Vesicle Exocytosis
at Central Excitatory Synapses

Synapse is the membrane contact area for information transfer between two neurons, or a neuron and an effector (like gland or muscle cell). In the case of a chemical synapse, it does the job by releasing vesicle-stored neuron transmitters, which can induce the activation of postsynaptic receptors. Decades of studies have revealed three typical modes of vesicle exocytosis in neurons: Full-collapse, Kiss-and-run, and Compound exocytosis. This animation is trying to visualize each mode and their corresponding influence on postsynaptic cell.
